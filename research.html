Dissertation Research

Non-trophic Interactions in Bioenergetic Network Models

University of California, Davis

Advisor: Prof. Fernanda Valdovinos

June 2023 – Present

Developed novel framework classifying non-trophic interactions by habitat modifications and extended the Allometric Trophic Network model to multiplex networks to evaluate how facilitation, competition for space, and other non-consumptive interactions shape ecosystem stability across six ecological metrics. Applied multiplex network modeling to Chilean rocky intertidal communities across environmental gradients to identify how network structure and non-trophic interactions influence biodiversity patterns.

Estacion Costera de Investigaciones Marinas, Chile

Mentor: Dr. Evie Weiters

August 2025 – September 2025

Cell Therapy Clinical Pharmacology and Modeling Internship

Takeda Pharmaceuticals

Supervisor: Dr. Aman Singh

June 2024 – August 2024

Developed mathematical models of cellular dynamics during lymphodepletion chemotherapy administered to cancer patients before CAR-T cell treatments. Collaborated with European clinical consortium (T2Evolve) to integrate real-world clinical data into models of immune system dynamics, providing clinicians with quantitative tools to optimize chemotherapy dosing while elucidating underlying mechanisms of treatment response.

Undergraduate Research Experiences

Validation of Dynamical Systems Models for Vector-borne Virus Transmission in Honeybees

St. Olaf College | Advisor: Prof. Sara Clifton

Explored the impact of environmental conditions on viral transmission among bee hives with a two-part model to capture the deterministic within-hive and stochastic between-hive disease dynamics, resulting in a publication. Utilized model validation techniques to estimate parameters in increasingly complex stochastic mathematical models.

Modeling Climate Change's Impact on Macrocystis pyrifera

Arizona State University | Quantitative Research for the Life and Social Sciences Program (QRLSSP)

Advisors: Prof. Steffen Eikenberry and Prof. Claudia Pio Ferreira

Analyzed the impact of abiotic factors on the growth and survival of giant kelp forests off the coast of California by creating a non-autonomous ODE age-structured model. Communicated results of climate change's impact on giant kelp through weekly presentations and a final technical report.

Modeling Phage-Antibiotic Synergy Inhibited by Temperate and Chronic Virus Competition

St. Olaf College | Advisor: Prof. Sara Clifton

Modeled the efficacy of antibiotics when target bacteria are infected with a combination of temperate and chronic viruses, resulting in a publication.

Modeling Treatment Strategies for Transplant Patients

Indiana University-Purdue University Indianapolis | Research Experience for Undergraduates

Advisor: Prof. Julia Arciero

Analyzed the role of timing and dosage of alternative treatment strategies to immunosuppressants by modeling cellular dynamics of the immune system, resulting in a publication.
